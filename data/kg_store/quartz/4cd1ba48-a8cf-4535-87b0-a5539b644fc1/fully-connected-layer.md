# Fully-Connected Layer

Fully-connected layers are traditional neural network layers where each neuron is connected to every neuron in the previous layer. In CNNs, they are often used at the end of the network to combine high-level features extracted by convolutional and pooling layers for final classification or regression tasks. They can be reinitialized during transfer learning to adapt to new output classes.
