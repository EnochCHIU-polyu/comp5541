# Vanishing Gradient Problem

The vanishing gradient problem occurs in deep neural networks when gradients become extremely small as they are backpropagated through many layers, particularly with saturating activation functions like Sigmoid. This prevents weights in earlier layers from updating effectively, stalling training and making it difficult to learn deep representations.
