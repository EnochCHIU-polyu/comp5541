## Summary
Alphabet reported Q2 2025 revenues of $96.4 billion, up 14% YoY (13% constant currency). Operating income rose 14% to $31.3 billion, with an operating margin of 32.4%. Google Cloud revenues surged 32% to $13.6 billion, driven by GCP and AI solutions. Free cash flow improved to $24.8 billion for the quarter. EPS grew to $2.31 from $1.89.

## Overview
The quarter showcased robust momentum across all segments, with double-digit growth in Search, YouTube, subscriptions, and Cloud. AI integration is positively impacting the business, evidenced by strong performance of AI Overviews and AI Mode. Operating margins benefited from revenue growth and cost efficiencies, partially offset by a legal settlement charge. Capital expenditures increased to support Cloud and AI infrastructure.

## Further Direction
Alphabet plans to increase investment in AI and Cloud infrastructure given strong demand. Risks include legal settlements, currency fluctuations, and competitive pressures. Continued focus on cost discipline and innovation is expected to sustain growth, though margin expansion may moderate due to higher capex.
