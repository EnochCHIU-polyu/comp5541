## Summary
Alphabet delivered a strong Q1 2026 with consolidated revenues up 22% to $109.9B (19% constant currency), marking the 11th consecutive double-digit growth quarter. Google Cloud revenue surged 63% to $20.0B, with backlog nearly doubling to $460B. Operating margin expanded 2pp to 36.1%. Operating cash flow rose to $45.8B and free cash flow reached $10.1B for the quarter.

## Overview
Revenue growth was broad-based: Google Services +16% (Search +19%, YouTube ads +11%, subscriptions+19%), Cloud acceleration fueled by enterprise AI solutions. Operating income grew 30% to $39.7B. Non-GAAP constant currency growth of 19% shows strong underlying demand. Cloud backlog indicates sustained future revenue visibility. Capital expenditures remained high at $35.7B in Q1, reflecting heavy AI infrastructure investment.

## Further Direction
Alphabet is well-positioned to capitalize on AI monetization across Search, Cloud, and subscriptions. Continued investment in AI infrastructure and enterprise solutions will drive future growth. Key risks include competitive pressure in cloud and search, regulatory challenges, and potential macroeconomic headwinds. No explicit forward guidance was provided, but strong backlog and operational leverage suggest positive trajectory.
